Site Content

Overview

Content is the most powerful way to attract new customers. It is also the way we communicate with existing customers. It is paramount that it has clarity and consistency to build trust.

Content Guidelines

Site Banners

SEO Typography

These banners provide textual value for search engines. The copy should be keyword rich, but still structured so that it reads like text meant for a human.

Link Banner

This banner provides one or more links that will provide customers with inspiration and ideas on how to use the products below. Remember: any click that takes a customer further away from the product must provide added value for them to purchase later in their visit. Should be used sparingly.

Headline Banner

Standard inspirational image banner. It should be employed for either new product categories or where we believe our editorial imagery and copy will provide value beyond what the product imagery can provide.

Feature Banner

Very specific usage. These banners are for products that we feel are very special, but that the product imagery alone doesn't do justice. They can also be used where Pier 1 feels additional information needs to be presented to help the customer make a buying decision.

SEO Footer (coming soon)

These banners provide textual value for search engines. The copy should be keyword rich, but still structured so that it reads like text meant for a human. Since they are the final item on the page, these may also provide links to related categories or editorial content.

Embedded Grid Content

These content block should be used thoughfully and deliberately. There is potential to move customers out of the buying path. Also, over use can quickly result in banner blindness[1].

Usage Priority

  1. Cross-selling – Near the bottom of a grid page to provide additional categories that may prevent a site exit.
    Example: Cushions near furniture
  2. Middle of the Funnel Offers – Helps the customer who may be struggling with making a buying decision
    Example: Discount with end date
  3. Inspirational Content – Presented with the goal of increasing AOV by showing ways use or accessorize.
    Example: Glitter Strings near vases

Three Variations

Visual Filters

Visual filters are a high-affordance method for customers to quickly narrow a product grid down to just the products they are interested in.

  • When possible use icons—images tend to set an expectation in the user's mind
  • Keep the buttons large (48px or 64px)
  • The top banner should only be added if additional explaination is needed

  • Visual Filter Layouts:
  • Single
  • Multiple
  • Multiple + Text
